# IBNote 004 Everyday Activity Pack Foundation ADR

Status: Signed-off defaults for 004 implementation
Source of truth: `docs/features/004_everyday_activity_pack_foundation/prd.md`

## 1. Purpose

This document records fixed decisions for the 004 package so activity-pack expansion remains product-useful and does not drift into schoolization or quantity-only growth.

## ADR-001. Treat 004 as home-friendly inquiry/activity routine expansion, not template-count growth

- Status: accepted
- Context: the product needs broader everyday usefulness, but more templates alone do not guarantee that.
- Decision: 004 focuses on home-natural, repeatable inquiry/activity routines that create recording and revisit value.
- Consequences:
  - coverage matters more than count
  - one-off novelty tasks are lower priority

## ADR-002. Keep school-like curriculum structure out of the parent-facing pack

- Status: accepted
- Context: Unit-of-Inquiry structure and similar school mechanics feel heavy and unnatural in home use.
- Decision: exclude school-style curriculum packaging, lines of inquiry, rubric framing, and exhibition-like mechanics from 004.
- Consequences:
  - parent burden stays lower
  - the product remains a home companion rather than a teaching tool

## ADR-003. Favor repeatable routines over special projects

- Status: accepted
- Context: repeatable household routines produce stronger reuse and recording value than one-off “special activity” ideas.
- Decision: prioritize clusters that can be reused across many home moments.
- Consequences:
  - stronger long-term product reuse potential
  - better fit for IBNote's record/revisit loop

## ADR-004. Use cluster rationale and quality bar as the main selection discipline

- Status: accepted
- Context: pack expansion can become random if there is no selection discipline.
- Decision: every meaningful addition must be justified by cluster coverage and quality-bar fit.
- Consequences:
  - the package stays coherent
  - low-value or school-like additions are easier to reject

## ADR-005. Keep later/high-burden inquiry formats out of foundation scope unless explicitly promoted

- Status: accepted
- Context: some research-backed formats such as slow-burn personal inquiry or heavier mini-experiment packs may be valuable but not ideal for foundation scope.
- Decision: keep foundation scope focused on the most natural, lowest-burden clusters first.
- Consequences:
  - 004 remains more executable
  - future pack expansions retain clear room to grow
